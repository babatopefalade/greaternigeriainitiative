Foundation

Vision & Mission

I
Vision

A Nigeria that translates its endowments — human and natural resources — into durable power: industrially productive, technologically sovereign, militarily credible, and diplomatically consequential.

II
Mission

To develop, advance, and advocate for evidence-based policy frameworks across economic industrialisation, indigenous innovation, national security, and geopolitical strategy — rebuilding Nigeria's state capacity for sustained regional and global leadership.

Theory of Change

How Change Happens

01
Ideas Shape Policy Environments

Sustained, high-quality policy intellectual output — when strategically placed — shifts the Overton window and reframes what is considered politically feasible.

02
Networks Amplify Influence

A credible coalition of reform-oriented technocrats, academics, civil society actors, and diaspora leaders can create durable pressure for structural change even within resistant political systems.

03
Accountability Requires Data

Rigorous measurement of state performance — fiscal health, security outcomes, institutional quality — is a precondition for meaningful accountability and reform momentum.

Strategic Architecture

The Four Strategic Pillars

GNI's analytical and advocacy work is organised around four pillars constituting an integrated architecture of national power — analysed through the DIME framework.

⚙️
Industrialisation

Building Nigeria's productive base through manufacturing depth, value chain integration, and import substitution — converting raw resource endowment into durable economic power.

💡
Innovation

Cultivating indigenous technological capability, digital infrastructure, and knowledge economy institutions that reduce external dependency and generate sovereign intellectual capital.

🛡️
Military Might

Professionalising the armed forces, addressing civil-military relations deficits, and developing credible deterrence capacity commensurate with Nigeria's regional leadership ambitions.

🌍
Geopolitical Leverage

Deploying Nigeria's demographic weight, cultural influence, and institutional presence to lead in African diplomacy, shape multilateral agendas, and secure strategic interests abroad.

Analytical Framework
D — Diplomatic I — Informational M — Military E — Economic

All four pillars are analysed through the DIME framework, diagnosing gaps between Nigeria's capacity and ambition as a continental power.

Guiding Principles

How We Work

01Evidence Before Advocacy

GNI's policy positions will be grounded in empirical analysis, not ideological preference. Prescriptions must be traceable to evidence, and GNI will publicly acknowledge complexity and uncertainty where they exist.

02Long-Termism

Nigeria's structural challenges are generational. GNI resists the short-termism of electoral cycles and transactional politics, anchoring its work to 10–25 year strategic horizons.

03Institutional Independence

GNI does not align with political parties or factional interests. Its credibility depends on independence, and it will defend that independence against co-optation — including from state actors and commercial sponsors.

04Constructive Patriotism

GNI is critical without being cynical. It holds the Nigerian state to account not from a posture of despair, but from a conviction that transformation is achievable and from a commitment to building the intellectual infrastructure that makes it possible.

05Pan-African Solidarity

Nigeria's renewal is not an end in itself. GNI situates Nigerian strategy within a broader African development agenda, recognising that a stronger Nigeria is a precondition for a stronger continent.

Governance

Specialist Committees

Five thematic committees drive GNI's substantive work, each co-chaired by a practitioner and an academic to ensure analytical rigour and policy-relevant framing.

Committee I
Economic & Industrial Policy Committee

Oversees the industrialisation and innovation pillars; develops policy positions on manufacturing, trade, technology, and fiscal architecture.

Committee II
Security & Defence Advisory Committee

Drives the military might pillar; analyses civil-military relations, defence procurement, and internal security challenges.

Committee III
Foreign Policy & Geostrategy Committee

Leads the geopolitical leverage pillar; engages multilateral forums, shapes Nigeria's African diplomacy agenda, and advises on strategic partnerships.

Committee IV
Institutional Governance & Reform Committee

Addresses constitutional architecture, electoral integrity, federalism, and the structural conditions for effective statecraft.

Committee V
Research, Intelligence & Publications Committee

Coordinates GNI's knowledge output — policy briefs, working papers, data products, and public engagement — ensuring coherence across the Initiative's strategic agenda.

Founding Statement
Nigeria does not lack resources, people, or potential. It lacks the sustained intellectual and institutional architecture to convert these assets into strategic outcomes. The Greater Nigeria Initiative is founded to begin building that architecture — brick by brick, brief by brief, generation by generation.

We do not promise quick victories. We offer rigorous analysis, frank counsel, and a long-term commitment to the Nigeria that should be.
